Unknown Network Traffic

Overview

This use case addresses the detection and investigation of network traffic that does not immediately align with known, expected, or documented behavior within the environment. Unknown traffic may indicate benign misconfigurations, new applications, or malicious activity such as command-and-control (C2), data exfiltration, or reconnaissance.

Attack Description

Adversaries frequently leverage unfamiliar protocols, uncommon ports, or unexpected destinations to evade detection. Unknown network traffic may be associated with:

  • Command-and-control communications
  • Data exfiltration over non-standard channels
  • Lateral movement using uncommon protocols
  • Unauthorized remote access tools or backdoors
  • Reconnaissance or beaconing activity

What to Look For

  • Traffic using unfamiliar or non-standard protocols
  • Connections over unexpected or high-numbered ports
  • Encrypted traffic with unknown or suspicious certificates
  • Unexpected outbound connections to external IPs or domains
  • Persistent or periodic traffic with no clear business purpose

Investigation Workflow

Unknown Traffic

Identify Traffic Characteristics

  • What protocol is being used?
    • Standard protocols (HTTP, HTTPS, DNS, SMB): Review legitimacy
    • Non-standard or custom protocols: Flag for deeper analysis
    • Encrypted traffic: Note encryption method and certificate details
  • What ports are involved?
    • Standard ports: Verify expected service
    • Non-standard ports: Investigate purpose
    • High-numbered ports: Check for potential backdoor

Identify Source and Destination

  • Is the source internal?
    • Identify asset and owner
    • Check asset baseline behavior
    • Review recent changes to the system
  • Is the destination internal?
    • Verify destination is an authorized service
    • Confirm the destination should accept this traffic
  • Is the destination external?
    • Check reputation of external IP or domain
    • Review threat intelligence feeds
    • Identify geolocation and hosting provider

Is This Traffic Authorized?

  • Yes
    • Document business justification
    • Update asset inventory and behavioral baseline
    • Create an exception if traffic is recurring
    • Close the alert
  • No
    • Check volume and frequency of traffic
    • Analyze payload if possible
    • Determine if activity resembles data exfiltration, C2, or reconnaissance
    • Block traffic at the firewall

Does the Traffic Show Malicious Indicators?

  • Yes
    • Isolate affected system(s)
    • Capture full PCAP if possible
    • Escalate to Incident Response
    • Begin containment procedures
    • Block traffic at the firewall
  • No, but suspicious
    • Request senior analyst review
    • Continue enhanced monitoring
    • Consider blocking at firewall or proxy
    • Document activity for pattern analysis

Tools & Queries

  • Elastic Security (network, flow, proxy, and endpoint telemetry)
  • Firewall and proxy logs
  • Packet capture tools (tcpdump, Wireshark)
  • Threat intelligence platforms
  • Asset inventory and CMDB

Response Workflow

  • Validate whether traffic is authorized or malicious
  • Contain or block confirmed malicious traffic
  • Isolate impacted assets when required
  • Engage Incident Response for confirmed threats
  • Document findings, decisions, and actions taken
