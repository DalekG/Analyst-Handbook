After-Hours Privileged Access

Overview

Privileged access outside of standard business hours is a high-risk indicator. While often associated with emergency maintenance or on-call duties, it is also a primary signature of credential compromise, as adversaries often operate during low-staffing periods to evade detection and manual intervention.

Attack Description

Adversaries leverage administrative credentials during "quiet" hours to perform high-impact actions with a lower risk of being noticed by the account owner. Common activities include:

  • Mass Data Staging: Moving large volumes of data to exfiltration points.
  • System Reconfiguration: Disabling security agents or modifying firewall rules.
  • Persistence: Creating new administrative accounts or backdoors.
  • Ransomware Deployment: Executing encryption routines when IT staff response is delayed.

Investigation Workflow

Identify Access Details

Specific account involved:

  • Named Administrator: Facilitates direct contact and verification.
  • Generic Admin Account: Identify the specific individual currently checked out or using the account via PAM logs.
  • Service Account: High Concern: Service accounts should typically not have interactive (RDP/SSH) login capabilities.
  • Break-Glass Account: Verify if an official emergency has been declared.

Time of access:

  • Late Evening: Possible legitimate overtime; check for active project deadlines.
  • Middle of Night (2-5 AM): High suspicion; typical "window of opportunity" for attackers.
  • Weekend/Holiday: Verify against the on-call rotation or pre-approved maintenance windows.

Identify Access Source and Method

Source location:

  • Corporate VPN: More likely legitimate; verify the VPN session IP and MFA status.
  • Direct Internet: High concern; indicates bypass of standard secure access gateways.
  • Known Admin Workstation: Lower concern; verify the host's physical location.
  • Unknown System: Investigate immediately as a potential "bring your own device" (BYOD) or rogue host.
  • Foreign Geolocation: Very high concern unless the administrator is on verified international travel.

Access method:

  • Standard Protocols (RDP/SSH): Verify if this matches the user's typical remote management style.
  • Unusual Protocols: (e.g., PowerShell Remoting from a new host) Investigate for "Living-off-the-Land" tactics.
  • Brute Force Pattern: Multiple failed attempts followed by a success suggests a credential compromise event.

Verify Legitimacy

Contact Account Owner Directly:

  • Contact via phone or secure IM (Do not use email, as the account may be compromised).
  • Confirm they are currently working and have accessed the specific target system.
  • Inquire about the specific business purpose of the activity.

Check for Authorization:

  • Review Change Management (CM) tickets or scheduled maintenance windows.
  • Verify the administrator's status on the official On-Call schedule.
  • Check for documented emergency manager approvals.

Is Access Authorized?

  • Yes (Verified with owner or approved change):
    • Monitor the session activity to ensure it stays within the stated scope.
    • Document the authorization details and ticket number.
    • Close the alert once the work is confirmed complete.
  • Cannot Verify (User unresponsive):
    • Request Senior Analyst review and begin technical behavior analysis.
    • Attempt alternate contact methods (e.g., calling their manager).
    • Prepare to disable the account if any destructive or suspicious actions are observed.
  • No (User denies access or no authorization found):
    • Disable the account immediately.
    • Terminate all active sessions for the user globally.
    • Execute the Account Compromise Workflow.
    • Perform a full audit of all actions taken during the session.

Tools & Queries

  • Privileged Access Management (PAM) logs (CyberArk, BeyondTrust)
  • VPN/MFA Authentication logs (Duo, Okta, Azure AD)
  • SIEM Search: EventCode=4624 Logon_Type=10 (RDP) filtered by Time_Range
  • EDR Process Trees (to see what scripts/tools were launched in the session)

Response Workflow

  • Revoke access for unauthorized sessions immediately.
  • Identify any "backdoor" accounts or persistence created during the window.
  • Escalate confirmed unauthorized access to Incident Response (IR).
  • Update conditional access policies to further restrict off-hours privileged logins.
