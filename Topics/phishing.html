Phishing Email Indicators

Overview

Phishing remains the primary initial access vector for adversaries in 2025. This workflow provides a structured approach to analyzing suspicious emails, identifying malicious attachments or links, and determining the scope of a potential credential harvest or malware delivery attempt.

Attack Description

Adversaries use deceptive emails to trick users into revealing sensitive information or executing malicious code. Common techniques include:

  • Business Email Compromise (BEC): Impersonating executives or vendors to authorize fraudulent payments.
  • Credential Harvesting: Deceptive links to fake login portals (O365, Okta, VPN).
  • Malware Delivery: Attachments (ISO, LNK, ZIP, HTML) containing first-stage loaders.
  • Quishing: Using QR codes to bypass traditional email gateway URL filters.

Investigation Workflow

Analyze Email Headers

Sender Authenticity

  • Check Sender Address: Is the "Friendly From" name consistent with the actual email address?
  • Review Domain: Look for look-alike domains (e.g., rnicrosoft.com vs. microsoft.com).
  • Authentication Results: Verify SPF, DKIM, and DMARC status. A "Fail" or "Softfail" increases suspicion.

Analyze Content & Links

Deceptive Characteristics

  • Urgency/Threats: Does the email demand immediate action (e.g., "Account Lockout", "Unpaid Invoice")?
  • Hyperlinks: Hover over links to reveal the true destination. Is the URL shortened or hosted on a suspicious TLD (.top, .xyz, .pw)?
  • Brand Spoofing: Are logos or signatures slightly off-color or low-resolution?

Analyze Attachments

Suspicious File Types

  • Archives: (ZIP, 7Z, RAR) especially if password-protected to evade gateway scanning.
  • Disk Images: (ISO, IMG, VHD) used to bypass Mark-of-the-Web (MotW) controls.
  • HTML/HTM: Often used for "Local" phishing or redirecting to credential harvesters.
  • Office Docs: Check for macros or embedded external references.

Determine Scope & Impact

Has the threat spread?

  • Recipient List: Was this a targeted "Spear Phish" or a broad campaign sent to many users?
  • User Interaction: Check proxy/web logs. Did any user click the link or download the file?
  • Authentication Logs: Following a click, was there a successful login from an unusual IP?

Tools & Queries

  • Email Gateway Logs (Proofpoint, Mimecast, Defender for Office 365)
  • Header Analysis Tools (MXToolbox, MessageHeader)
  • URL/File Sandboxes (VirusTotal, Joe Sandbox, Any.Run)
  • SIEM Search: sourcetype="email_logs" recipient_address="user@company.com"

Response Workflow

  • Purge: Remove the malicious email from all user inboxes globally.
  • Block: Blacklist the sender's address, domain, and any identified URLs at the firewall/proxy.
  • Contain: Isolate any host where an attachment was executed or a link was clicked.
  • Credential Hygiene: Force password resets and revoke MFA tokens for users who interacted with the email.
  • Awareness: If a broad campaign, issue a "Phish Alarm" notification to the organization.
