Unusual Email

Overview

This use case focuses on identifying and investigating email messages that exhibit unusual, suspicious, or potentially malicious characteristics. Unusual emails may be part of phishing campaigns, malware delivery, credential harvesting, or social engineering attempts.

Attack Description

Email remains a primary initial access vector for adversaries. Malicious emails may contain links, attachments, or social engineering content designed to deceive users into interacting with malicious infrastructure or executing malware.

  • Phishing and spear-phishing attempts
  • Malware delivery via attachments
  • Credential harvesting via malicious URLs
  • Business Email Compromise (BEC)
  • Social engineering and pretexting

What to Look For

  • Unexpected sender or spoofed display name
  • Suspicious sender domain or reply-to address
  • Urgent or coercive language
  • Unexpected attachments or embedded URLs
  • Email failing SPF, DKIM, or DMARC checks

Investigation Workflow

Unusual Email

Initial Email Analysis

  • Review email headers for anomalies (SPF, DKIM, DMARC results)
  • Inspect sender domain and compare against known legitimate domains
  • Perform domain reputation and WHOIS lookup
  • Perform IP reputation and geolocation lookup on sending infrastructure
  • Review email body for social engineering indicators

Contains URL or Attachment?

  • Yes
    • Analyze URL using VirusTotal or other approved analysis tools
    • Compute hash of attachment (MD5/SHA256)
    • Submit attachment hash to VirusTotal or other approved tools
    • Do not open or execute attachments directly
  • No
    • Request senior CND analyst review
    • Notify and thank the reporting user

Malicious URL?

  • Yes
    • Verify that the user did not click the URL
    • Collect all relevant artifacts (URL, headers, sender, timestamps)
    • Prepare a cyber 9-line report
    • Request senior CND analyst review
    • Escalate the incident
  • No
    • Request senior CND analyst review
    • Notify and thank the reporting user

Malicious Attachment Hash?

  • Yes
    • Verify that the attachment was not downloaded or opened
    • Collect all relevant artifacts (hashes, filename, headers)
    • Prepare a cyber 9-line report
    • Request senior CND analyst review
    • Escalate the incident
  • No
    • Request senior CND analyst review
    • Notify and thank the reporting user

Tools & Queries

  • Email security gateway and mail logs
  • Email header analysis tools
  • VirusTotal and approved malware analysis platforms
  • Domain and IP reputation services
  • Endpoint detection and response (EDR)

Response Workflow

  • Determine whether email is malicious or benign
  • Contain malicious emails through blocking or quarantine
  • Verify user interaction and assess impact
  • Escalate confirmed incidents to Incident Response
  • Document findings and notify stakeholders
