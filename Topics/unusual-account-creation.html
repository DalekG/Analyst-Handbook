Unusual Account Creation

Overview

Unauthorized or unusual account creation is a common technique used by adversaries to establish persistence within a network. This workflow ensures that every new account is scrutinized for proper authorization, appropriate privilege levels, and legitimate business justification.

Attack Description

Adversaries create accounts to maintain access (Persistence), escalate privileges, or bypass security controls. Tactics often include:

  • Creating local administrator accounts for "backdoor" access
  • Adding users to sensitive groups (e.g., Domain Admins)
  • Misusing service accounts to hide malicious automated processes
  • Creating accounts during non-business hours to avoid detection

What to Look For

  • Accounts created outside of standard HR onboarding windows
  • New accounts with immediate elevation to administrative groups
  • Account names that mimic existing naming conventions (e.g., "svc_backup2")
  • Accounts created by users who do not typically perform administrative tasks
  • Successful logins immediately following account creation

Investigation Workflow

Identify Account Details

What type of account?

  • Local Administrator: High concern; investigate immediately
  • Domain User: Standard concern; verify against HR/Onboarding
  • Service Account: Verify legitimacy with App/System owner
  • Guest/Temporary: Check for specific project authorization

What privileges were assigned?

  • Administrative Rights: Immediate review of group memberships
  • Standard User: Lower priority, but still requires validation
  • Specific Group Memberships: Verify necessity (e.g., Remote Desktop Users, VPN)

Identify Who Created the Account

Known Administrator?

  • Verify with the administrator directly (Use phone or IM; do not use email if compromise is suspected)
  • Check if a corresponding Change Management ticket exists
  • Confirm business justification for the new account

Unknown or Suspicious Creator?

  • Check if the creator's account itself has been compromised
  • Review all recent actions/logs performed by the creator account
  • Escalate immediately to Senior Analysts or IR

Identify Timing

When was the account created?

  • Business Hours:
    • More likely to be legitimate; still require verification of authorization
  • After Hours / Weekend / Holiday:
    • Higher suspicion; requires immediate verification
    • Check for other suspicious activity in the same timeframe (logins, file access)

Is the Account Creation Authorized?

  • Yes (Verified ticket/New Hire/Service):
    • Document the justification and ticket number
    • Verify the account follows the principle of least privilege
    • Close the alert
  • No:
    • Disable the account immediately
    • Review all activity performed by the account since creation
    • Scan for additional unauthorized accounts created by the same source
    • Escalate to Incident Response (IR)
    • Initiate a compromise investigation on the creator's account

Tools & Queries

  • Active Directory Users and Computers (ADUC) / Azure AD Audit Logs
  • SIEM (Filter for Event IDs: 4720 - Account Created, 4732/4728 - Group Membership)
  • Change Management System (ServiceNow, Jira, etc.)
  • EDR (To track the process used to create local accounts)

Response Workflow

  • Identify and disable any unauthorized accounts
  • Identify the source of the creation (the 'actor')
  • Verify authorization via official documentation or direct contact
  • Perform impact analysis on any actions the new account took
  • Remediate the 'actor' account if it is found to be compromised
