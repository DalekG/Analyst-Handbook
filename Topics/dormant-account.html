Dormant Account Reactivation

Overview

Dormant accounts (those inactive for an extended period) are prime targets for adversaries. When reactivated outside of normal business processes, it is a significant indicator of account manipulation designed to provide a persistent, stealthy foothold within the environment.

Attack Description

Attackers leverage dormant accounts because they often go unmonitored and may still retain high-level permissions. Tactics include:

  • Gaining access via a separate breach and reactivating an old, forgotten account.
  • Social engineering helpdesk staff to reactivate a former employee's account.
  • Using compromised administrative credentials to re-enable an account and immediately change its password.

Investigation Workflow

Identify the Account Details

Type of account:

  • User Account: Check current employee status with HR (leave of absence, termination date).
  • Service Account: Verify if the associated service is still needed or operational.
  • Administrative Account: High concern; these accounts should rarely be dormant.
  • Vendor/Contractor Account: Check current contract status and end date.

How long was the account dormant?

  • Days: Lower concern; may be a simple oversight.
  • Weeks: Moderate concern.
  • Months/Years: High concern; suggests a targeted use of a forgotten asset.

What are the account privileges?

  • Standard User: Lower risk, but still requires investigation.
  • Elevated Privileges: High risk; immediate review needed.
  • Administrative/Root: Critical concern; assumes malicious intent until proven otherwise.

Identify Reactivation Details

Who/what reactivated the account?

  • HR System: Verify via HR processes/tickets.
  • Administrator: Verify directly with the specific administrator (phone/IM).
  • Automated Process: Check the legitimacy and configuration of the automation script.
  • Unknown: Investigate the source of the re-enable command immediately.

Authentication method post-reactivation:

  • Password: When was the password last changed? If it was changed right before use, suspicion increases.
  • Cached Credentials: How were old, cached credentials obtained and used?
  • Service Authentication: Verify the legitimacy of the service making the API calls.

Review Account Activity Since Reactivation

What has the account done?

  • Review all login locations and times (look for impossible travel).
  • Document systems accessed and network connections made.
  • Note files accessed or modified (especially sensitive data).
  • Audit commands or scripts executed via the session.
  • Verify usage of privileges—did the account attempt to elevate further?

Is the Reactivation Legitimate?

  • Yes (Employee return, contract extension, service reactivation):
    • Verify with the appropriate department (HR/Procurement/IT).
    • Confirm proper reactivation procedures were followed (e.g., password forced reset).
    • Verify current access needs match the assigned privileges (Least Privilege).
    • Document findings and close the alert.
  • No or Cannot Verify:
    • Disable the account immediately.
    • Force an immediate password reset (do not use the email associated with the account).
    • Review all activity for malicious actions or lateral movement.
    • Check for how the account credentials were obtained or leveraged.
    • Escalate to IR if compromise is indicated.
    • Investigate who/what had access to reactivate the account (internal threat?).

Tools & Queries

  • Active Directory/Azure AD Audit Logs (Event ID 4722/4723/4724 - Enable/Disable/Password Change)
  • HR Management System Logs (Workday, SAP)
  • SIEM Search: Filter for status disabled=false or enabled=true changes.
  • EDR Logs (Process execution and file access logs for post-reactivation activity)

Response Workflow

  • Disable the account and invalidate session tokens if unauthorized.
  • Engage IR procedures if data access or system compromise is confirmed.
  • Remediate the administrative weakness that allowed unauthorized reactivation.
  • Update identity and access management policies regarding dormant account lifecycle.
